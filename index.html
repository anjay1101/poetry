Rivers Flowed Strong

A long time ago
rivers flowed strong
with the leaves of
fall, fish danced
along the river nearly
leaping for joy,
enjoying the company of the
slithering Jesus-like
snakes in a time
before we found
God. Branches
polluted rivers. And
the only sounds were
those of all life:
mosquitos clamoring
& water rippling
against eroded rocks
with the occasional
tweet tweet of
a bird mocking
another one,
and flaming red
& bright blue dragon flies
chasing each-other across
the serene day.



And now, children dance
in the glitter of Sunday afternoon sunlight reflecting off the gentle September water.

But, with our petrol
plastic canoes, and
our glass and metal
cans, we are just skipping stones
across the broken bones
of a land that used
to be alive with life.

And,
there’s no telling how
much longer the river
will run for, before its
intoxication seeps all
life from the banks and
we’re left stranded with
no place for dogs to play &
no place for rain to stray and
no child will be able to say
they know what it means when a
river flows strong with life.

- Anjay Friedman

You never told me how to make a change

In cold October, hands get used to being
tucked into pockets, and sweaters colored like
the falls of the past flood the paths.

People learn to walk briskly away from
their fears again, no longer wandering with the weather,
and lips tighten, not willing to sacrifice any sliver of warmth.

Cold rain fills the streets, soaking socks falsely
protected by shoes, and feet remain as ghosts until

Humans rush past archaic wood frames into warmed air that
itself flees into the freedom of winter.

Darkness collapses like leaf covered sidewalks
slowly transform into dirty brown human creations.

And, branches burn when they hug the dried air
no choice between fight or flight.

· · · · ·

I left my window open, hoping for the cold
to invigorate change but then, found my hands
in pockets, a yellow sweater on, just one of a
crowd of scrambling birds.
