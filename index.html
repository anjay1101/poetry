Rivers Flowed Strong A long time ago rivers flowed strong with the leaves of fall, fish danced along the river nearly leaping for joy, enjoying the company of the slithering Jesus-like snakes in a time before we found God. Branches polluted rivers. And the only sounds were those of all life: mosquitos clamoring & water rippling against eroded rocks with the occasional tweet tweet of a bird mocking another one, and flaming red & bright blue dragon flies chasing each-other across the serene day. And now, children dance in the glitter of Sunday afternoon sunlight reflecting off the gentle September water. But, with our petrol plastic canoes, and our glass and metal cans, we are just skipping stones across the broken bones of a land that used to be alive with life. And, there’s no telling how much longer the river will run for, before its intoxication seeps all life from the banks and we’re left stranded with no place for dogs to play & no place for rain to stray and no child will be able to say they know what it means when a river flows strong with life. - Anjay Friedman
